Short Bio

I am a Ph.D. student, currently working as a Graduate Research Assistant in the Department of Computer Science at the University of Alabama. I am working with Dr. Zhe Jiang in Spatial Data Mining research group.

I completed my bachelor's degree in Computer Engineering at Tribhuvan University, Nepal in 2011. I worked as a Software Engineer for around four years in E&T Co. Ltd. During my tenure in E&T, for the first two years, I was involved in developing tools to visualize and analyze fluid flow simulation data. We mostly used OpenGL and C++ for development. I was promoted as a Senior Software Engineer in 2013. I led a team of 8 engineers (computer and mechanical) to enhance the fluid flow simulation solver. We were able to improve the computation time by 9-fold. We also advanced the solver by implementing functionalities to address fluid flow characteristics such as turbulence and drag.

In The University of Alabama, I have been researching on spatial data mining problems. Over the past four years, I have published 4 peer-reviewed conference research papers ( SIGKDD 2018 2019, SSTD 2017, ACMSE 2017) and 4 journal papers (IEEE TKDE 2019, ACM TDS 2019, IEEE TIST 2019, IEEE TBD 2018).

I am currently seeking a full-time job opportunity in the field of data science.
